Solar Potential in India

India receives solar power of about 5000 trillion (5000 x 1018) kWh/year with about 300 clear sunshine days in a year. Average incident energy 4 to 7 kWh/m2/day with about 2300 - 3200 sunshine hours per year.

Photovoltaic

Photovoltaic (PV) effect is defined as the generation of an electromotive force as a result of the absorption of ionizing radiation. It appears as a potential difference (voltage) between two layers of a semiconductor slice in which the conductivities are opposite or between a semiconductor and a metal, under the effect of a light stream

Battery Info

As per IEA (International Energy Agency) recommendations, one should draw no more than 40% of a battery's rated energy capacity (VAh) during a 24hrs perriod.

Battery Condition

Generally for a six cell (12V) lead-acid batteries:

1. Open-circuit voltage after full charge 12.6V to 12.8V

2. Open-circuit voltage after full discharge 11.8V to 12.0V

3. Loaded at full discharge: 10.5V

4. Float charging 13.8V for gelled cells and 13.4V for wet cells

5. Typical (daily) charging : 14.2V to 14.5V

6. Equalisation charging (for wet lead-acid): 15V to 16V
